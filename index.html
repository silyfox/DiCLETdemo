1. Abstract

While the performance of cross-lingual TTS based on monolingual corpora has been significantly improved recently, generating cross-lingual speech still suffers from the foreign accent problem, leading to limited naturalness. Besides, current cross-lingual methods ignore modeling emotion, which is indispensable paralinguistic information in speech delivery. In this paper, we propose DiCLET-TTS, a Diffusion model based Cross-Lingual Emotion Transfer method that can transfer emotion from a source speaker to the intra-lingual or cross-lingual target speaker. Specifically, to relieve the foreign accent problem while improving the emotion expressiveness, the terminal distribution of the diffusion process is parameterized into a speaker-irrelevant but emotion-related linguistic prior by a prior text encoder with the emotion embedding as a condition. To address the weaker emotional expressiveness problem caused by speaker disentanglement in emotion embedding, a novel orthogonal projection based emotion disentangling module (OP-EDM) is proposed to learn the speaker-irrelevant but emotion discriminative emotion embedding. Moreover, to effectively guide the speaker and emotion in the generation process, a condition-enhanced DPM decoder is introduced to strengthen the modeling ability of the speaker and the emotion in the reverse diffusion process. Extensive experiments have been conducted on five monolingual corpora. Compared to state-of-the-art methods, the DiCLET-TTS gets the best performance on both cross-lingual and emotion transfer tasks, indicating that the DiCLET-TTS can synthesize reasonable Chinese emotional speech for the intra-lingual and cross-lingual target speakers, and the advantage of OP-EDM in learning disentangled emotion embedding for emotion transfer.
